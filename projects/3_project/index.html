Gspeed 2017

FASE chassis; Technical Lead; First prize of FSC 2017

In 2017, I served as the technical lead of the Gspeed Formula Racing Team (Gspeed). Meanwhile, I was responsible for developing the entire chassis of Gspeed 2017, including suspension, steering, braking and transmission systems.

Gspeed 2017 (left) and its chassis (right).
Powertrain, aerodynamics and cockpit.

Inspired by Formula One (F1), we introduced the 3rd-spring technology in Gspeed 2017. It can decouple the chassis’s longitudinal and lateral movements, improving the handling stability in the two directions, which was validated in Adams/Car simulations.

Simulations of the 3rd-spring chassis: bounce, roll, steering ratio, etc.

The maneuver of the full vehicle under transient and steady conditions was studied too. The virtual prototype was verified in some standard experiments, such as the skidpad, and was also “driven” on the 1:1 Xiangyang circuit!

Full vehicle simulation in Adams/Car.

In addition to rich simulations, we invested much effort in real hardware experiments and obtained a lot of valuable data.

Component experiments on dampers, tires, springs, and wheel hubs.
System-level experiments: K&C testing, powertrain bench testing, and wind tunnel testing.

The completed Gspeed 2017 looks so aggressive and exquisite!

In 2017, I witnessed the growth of an excellent R&D team (approximately 100 people) and proudly worked with every member.

In Oct 2017, the Gspeed race car was shipped to Xiangyang circuit for Formula Student China (FSC) 2017, where there were 72 domestic universities to pariciapte in. We finally won the 6th place (1st prize) of overall events, 2nd place of static events, and 1st place in Engineering Design.
